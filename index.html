The Sun

The Sun is a gigantic star that is the heart to our solar system and is responsible for Earth’s climate and weather.

All the Sun’s energy is generated from its core, by nuclear fusion, as Hydrogen is converted to Helium. The temperature inside the sun can reach up to 15 million degrees Celsius and the temperature on the surface of the Sun is around 5,600 degrees Celsius. Although the Sun is special to us, there are billions of stars just like our sun across the Milky Way Galaxy.

  • Light from the sun takes just 8 minutes to reach Earth
  • 1 million Earths could fit inside the sun
  • The Sun is 4.5 billion years old
  • The Sun generates solar wind
  • The Sun, one day, will consume the Earth

Mercury

The smallest and closest planet to the sun, its lack of atmosphere makes mercury hot by day and cold by night.

Mercury rotates exactly three times for every two orbits. Because of this behaviour, this is what makes its days last twice as long as its year. It contains a large iron core, which generates a magnetic field. The surface is rocky and is made mostly of volcanic lava flows pockmarked by impact craters.

  • Mercury is 57.9 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is 4222.6 hours
  • The diameter of Mercury is 4869 km
  • The length of one year is 88.0 earth days
  • The rotation period is 1407.6 hours

Venus

Venus - the second planet closest to the sun - is almost the size of Earth that has a dense and cloudy atmosphere that hides the surface from view.

To map the surface of Venus from orbit you have to use radar to penetrate the clouds. Venus spins very slowly backwards, hence the negative sign in its rotation period, although its clouds rotate 60 times faster. Its surface is mostly lava flows, which are much younger than those on Mercury.

  • Venus is 108.2 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is 2802.0 hours
  • The diameter of Venus is 12,104 km
  • The length of one year is 224.7 earth days
  • The rotation period is -5832.5 hours

Earth

The planet we live on. The only planet to have abundant liquid water today and an atmosphere that we can breathe.

Earth is the third planet from tbe sun. Its core generates a magnetic field. Its surface is divided into plates that grow by spreading at mid-ocean ridges and slide below the edges of continents, which cause many earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. Earth is the only known home to life, which in turn has hugely changed this planet.

  • Earth is 149.6 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is 24 hours
  • The diameter of Earth is 12,756 km
  • The length of one year is 365.2 earth days
  • The rotation period is 23.9 hours

Mars

A world that had liquid water long ago, but has changes to a cold, dry climate.

Mars - the fourth planet from the sun - has canyons, valleys, giant volcanoes and dusty plains. The hunt is on to find out whether there was any life there in the distant path when conditions were more suitable, and even whether and microbes were still live there today. Mars lost its ancient magnetic field and its early atmosphere.

  • Mars is 227.9 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is 24.7 hours
  • The diameter of Mars is 6792 km
  • The length of one year is 687.0 earth days
  • The rotation period is 24.6 hours

Jupiter

Jupiter Great Red Spot is a storm that has raged since at least the nineteenth century. The size of the Giant Red Spot is shrinking now, but still larger than earth.

Jupiter - the fifth planet from the sun - is the largest planet in the Solar System, with a mass about two and a half times of that of the other seven planets combined. Jupiter has played a key role in the evolution of the Solar System, sweeping up debris and affecting the orbits of asteroids, comets and other planets.

  • Jupiter is 778.6 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is just 9.9 hours
  • The diameter of Jupiter is 142,984 km
  • The length of one year is 4331 earth days
  • The rotation period is 10 hours

Saturn

Saturn - the sixth planet from the sun - is best known for its beautiful rings, but they might not have always been such prominent rings.

Saturn is famous for its beautiful rings, easily seen through a small telescope from Earth and made most entirely of water ice, with only a little rock and dust. The diameter of the main rings is about 280,000 km, but they are incredibly thin, averaging only 20 m thick.

  • Saturn is 1433.5 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is just 10.7 hours
  • The diameter of Saturn is 120,536 km
  • The length of one year is 10,747 earth days
  • The rotation period is 10.7 hours

Uranus

Uranus is a planet tipped over on its side.

Uranus, the seventh planet from the sun, is a cold and windy planet. The planet us surrounded by 13 faint ice rings and 27 small moons that rotate at a near 90-degree angle from the planet on its orbit. This is what causes Uranus to spin on its side and orbit the sun like a rolling ball.

  • Uranus is 2872.5 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is just 17.2 hours
  • The diameter of Uranus is 50,188 km
  • The length of one year is 30,589 earth days
  • The rotation period is 17 hours

Neptune

Neptune – the eighth planet from the sun – is named after the Roman god of the sea.

Neptune is a dark and cold planet that is blasted by supersonic winds and sits as the most distant planet in our solar system – 30 times as far from the sun as Earth. Large storms surge through the upper atmosphere of Neptune and winds track around the planet at around 600 meters per second.

  • Uranus is 4495.1 million km from the sun
  • The length of one day is just 16.1 hours
  • The diameter of Uranus is 49,528 km
  • The length of one year is 59,800 earth days
  • The rotation period is 16 hours
